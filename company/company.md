# DriveEasy Car Rental

## Overview
DriveEasy Car Rental is a national car rental company that puts customers on the
road from branches at **airports, downtown locations, and suburbs** across the
country. Led by CEO **Elena Reyes**, DriveEasy rents a full range of vehicle
classes — economy, compact, SUV, luxury, and van — each fitted with GPS
telematics for location, mileage, fuel, and diagnostics. Every rental follows the
same simple lifecycle: **reserve → pickup → in-use → return → invoice**.

## How it works
DriveEasy is organized as a set of **divisions**, each owning one part of the
business and working off shared platforms — the **booking system**, the **pricing
engine**, branch counter terminals, the airport shuttle, and the return lot.

- **Regions and branches** deliver the service on the ground. The country is split
  into regions, each region into groups of branches, and each branch runs its
  counters and lot. This is where customers are actually checked in and out.
- **Central functions** keep every branch supplied and solvent — they buy and move
  the fleet, set fair prices, design the customer journey, and handle money and
  compliance — so local teams can focus on the customer in front of them.

## Purpose & goals
- Get every customer into the right vehicle quickly, at a fair and transparent price.
- Keep the fleet reliable, clean, and available where demand actually is.
- Make each branch consistent, so a DriveEasy rental feels the same everywhere.
- Grow responsibly by reinvesting in vehicles, technology, and people.

## Vision
Effortless mobility for everyone, everywhere.

## Mission
Get every customer on the road quickly with a reliable, fairly-priced vehicle and
friendly service.

## Divisions
DriveEasy delivers all of this through five divisions. See
[divisions/divisions.md](divisions/divisions.md) for the full index and boundaries.

- **Rental Operations** — runs the regions, branches, and counters that serve customers.
- **Fleet Management** — buys, maintains, and balances the vehicles.
- **Customer Experience** — owns the booking journey, support, and loyalty.
- **Finance & Administration** — handles pricing economics, billing, and compliance.
- **Technology** — builds and maintains the platform software, and fixes its bugs.
