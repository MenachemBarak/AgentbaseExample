# Role — Chief Technology Officer (role_cto)

## Definition
The **CTO** leads DriveEasy's **Technology** division and owns all of the company's
software. This role is held by **Nadia Brandt**. She reports to CEO **Elena Reyes**
and sits above the **Platform Engineering** team. Unusually for a C-level role, she
is also an **active code contributor** — she reviews and merges changes to the
platform repos herself.

## Responsibilities
- Own the health and roadmap of the platform repos: `booking-system`,
  `pricing-engine`, `counter-terminal`, and `fleet-telematics`.
- Set engineering standards — tests, environments, review, and release safety.
- Own DriveEasy's **agentic transformation** of platform work: decide which bridges
  exist (e.g. the bug-solving squad) and how they map to the engineering roles.
- Approve production releases and command Sev-1 incident response.
- Partner with the other divisions to turn their needs into shipped software.

## Purpose
Nadia exists to make the rental platform reliable and fast to change — so a bug
reported at a branch counter becomes a verified fix in production quickly, and the
features the business asks for ship without breaking what already works.
